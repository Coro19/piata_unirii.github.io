History of the buildings


Each buildings with its own unique history

After the turks lost control of the city, time for modernization has started and one of the first places in the city to see the new era of contruction was Union Square.

The first buildings to be constructed were the Catholic Dome(1736-1774), the Serbian Orthodox Cathedral(1744-1748), the Holy Trinity Monument(1739-1740) and the Baroque Palace(1752-1754).

A Short List


Catholic Cathedral

The construction was split into two stages, the first one from 1736 until 1751, and the second stage started in 1755 and was finalized in 1774. It was built in rococo and baroque styles, decorated by the biggest names from Austria. The building has just been opened again, after long and extensive renovations.


Serbian Orthodox Cathedral

Sitting on the opposite side of the square from the Catholic Cathedra, this was built in just four years, from 1744 until 1748.


"Nikolaus Lenau" High School

This building was finished in 1761. Until the high school was founded in 1870, the building was used as a theatre.


Baroque Palace

Used in 1733 as the headquarters for the mining office, the palace has seen signifficant transformation from 1752 until 1754 , another reconstruction in 1774, which added a new wing in the direction of the main square and created two inner courtyards. In 1885-1886, architect Jacques Klein removed the baroque elements and the wing separating the two courtyards was demolished.


Bruck House

This building sitting next to the Baroque Palace was built in 1910 by architect László Székely in Art Nuveau and Secession styles.
