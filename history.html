Union Square


A place with a rich history

This place hosts some of the oldest buildings still standing in Timisoara, its history dating back to the 16th century/

It has seen a lot of changes throughout the years, being called Hauptplatz( Main Square) and later Domplatz(The Square of the Dome).

Brief History


18th century

After the city was eliberated from the turksin 1716, the square has obtained its current form, being used for a long time as a market. In 1733 projects to renew the square have been proposed and and in 1736 the construction of the Catholic Cathedral was started.


19th century

On the 1st of November 1846, piano player Franz Liszt arrived in Timisoara in what was the last tour of his career. On the 2nd of November, he played in the festive hall of the city hall, which today is building of "Nikolaus Lenau" High School.


20th century

After the retreat of serbian troops on August 3rd 1919, romanian troops marched towards Union Square, where the ceremony of the union of Banat region with Romania took place. This event gives us the current name of the square. In the 80s romanian architect È˜erban Sturdza contributed to the modernizing of the square.
